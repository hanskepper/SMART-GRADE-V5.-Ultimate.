1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

However, SMART GRADE does NOT use traditional browser cookies. Instead, we use the browser's localStorage API for storing your data. This is a more modern and secure approach.

Important: SMART GRADE does not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or any third-party cookies that follow you across websites.

2. How We Use localStorage

SMART GRADE uses localStorage instead of traditional cookies. localStorage is a web storage mechanism that allows websites to store data directly in your browser. Here's what we store:

User Authentication

Your login session and preferences

Academic Data

Grades, subjects, coefficients, goals

App Preferences

Theme, font, language settings

Activity Data

History, notifications, achievements

3. Types of Storage We Use

SMART GRADE uses the following storage mechanisms:

  • localStorage: Stores all your application data permanently until you delete it or clear your browser data.
  • sessionStorage: Temporarily stores transfer codes (expire after 10 minutes).
  • No traditional cookies: We do not use HTTP cookies for tracking or advertising.
No Tracking: We do not use analytics cookies, marketing cookies, or any form of cross-site tracking.

4. LocalStorage vs Cookies

More Secure

localStorage is not sent to servers with every request

Larger Storage

Up to 10MB per domain (vs 4KB for cookies)

No Expiry

Data persists until manually cleared

No Tracking

Cannot be read by third parties

5. Third-Party Services

SMART GRADE uses the following third-party services that may set their own storage:

GitHub Gist

Used for cloud transfer (optional). May set its own authentication cookies.

Google Fonts

Used for displaying custom fonts. May collect IP addresses.

Font Awesome

Used for icons. May set cookies for CDN optimization.

These services are used only when you explicitly use the corresponding features (cloud transfer, etc.).

6. Managing Your Storage

You have full control over your data stored by SMART GRADE:

  • Clear localStorage: You can delete all your data by deleting your account or clearing browser storage.
  • Export Data: Use the Export feature to save a backup before clearing.
  • Disable Cloud Features: You can choose not to use cloud transfer/backup features.
  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to clear localStorage through privacy settings.
Recommendation: Before clearing your browser data, use the Export feature to save a backup of your grades and achievements.

7. Storage Duration

Different types of data have different retention periods:

Account Data

Until account deletion or manual clearing

Transfer Codes

10 minutes (automatically deleted)

Cloud Backups

24 hours (optional feature)

Preferences

Until manually changed
